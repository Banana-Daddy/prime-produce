§ 02 — The Trade

A small broker,
patiently kept.

Prime Produce began in 1999 with two principals, one phone line, and a simple standard: call the grade you see, ship the load you called, and settle on the terms you signed. Twenty‑six years later the practice is the same. The grades are confirmed on the dock. The specs travel with the load. The receivables are protected under PACA.

The trade is built on repeat counterparties — customer accounts, growers and shippers, transporters, and other brokers who work the same year in, year out. We don’t pitch. We answer. A good partner is a short call and a clean tally.

“Supply from Mexico, the East Coast, and Chile continues to move — weather patterns shift, but the obligation to deliver on spec does not.”

Marc Nelson, Partner  ·  Blue Book Services, “Hat’s Off to Dallas,” 2016

Who we
do trade with.

i.

Customer Accounts

Retailers, foodservice distributors, and restaurant groups buying fruit and vegetables at wholesale. You get the grade you specified, in the pack size you specified, on the day you specified. If we can’t, we call.

Wholesale · Foodservice · Retail

ii.

Growers & Shippers

Domestic farms and Mexican partners looking for a broker who moves the crop and pays on the contract. Clean communication from field report through final accounting. Receivables protected under PACA.

Carriers and owner‑operators pulling reefer loads out of DFW and the Pharr / McAllen corridor. Clear specs, clean pickup windows, prompt settlement. The load paperwork is ready when you are.
